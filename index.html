What are Vericose Veins ?

All veins have valves to prevent the blood from flowing in reverse. When these valves get damaged, the veins become large and twisted due to the pressure of the backward-moving blood. Such damaged veins are called varicose veins.

Varicose veins are dangerous to neglect. If your damaged veins are not closed with the correct treatment, the pressure of the backward-flowing blood damages the healthy veins as well. The longer you avoid treatment, the worse your condition will become. There’s also a risk of the veins bursting open and causing major blood loss..

Symptoms

Sensations of irritation, burning or itching.

Swelling of the legs.

Changes in skin colour or texture.

Ulcers that take a long time to heal.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the risks of varicose veins treatment?
Our varicose veins treatments are quite safe and are performed by experienced doctors. However, in rare cases, the treatment can cause nerve damage and infections.
What is the recovery time for varicose veins treatment?
At our varicose hospital, patients begin walking within a few hours of treatment and can return home on the same day. You can expect to make a full recovery within a few weeks.
How to prepare for varicose veins treatment?
Communicate all of your concerns with our doctors. Make sure you inform them about all the medicines you are taking. Eat a healthy diet, drink plenty of water and stop smoking at least a week before your treatment.
Is the ultrasound doppler screening free at Avis Hospitals?
The doppler test for varicose veins cost is ₹4500, but you can get it for free at our varicose veins hospital. The doppler screening is free only for the first consultation, and is only conducted if the doctor deems it necessary for diagnosis.
What to do to prevent varicose veins?
Varicose veins cannot be avoided completely, especially if your family has a history of the issue. However, exercising daily, avoiding long periods of sitting or standing and a healthy diet can slow down the development of varicose veins.
